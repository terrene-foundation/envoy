# Public-facing claim-verification sweep

**Status:** DRAFT for Phase-00 exit gate (ROADMAP §"Envoy-claim verification" — both bullets).
**Audience:** envoy maintainers + Foundation board (board sees the redacted summary in the one-pager).
**Source contract:** ROADMAP.md lines 16–17 — "Cross-check every factual claim destined for public Envoy collateral (Charter, README, website copy) for accuracy and supporting citation. Remove any comparative claims about other projects from public-facing documents; retain only neutral references where required for interoperability context (e.g. SKILL.md format)."
**Scope:** all public-facing files at repo root (CHARTER.md, README.md, DECISIONS.md, ROADMAP.md, NOTICE, CONTRIBUTING.md, SECURITY.md) plus the public-facing draft `workspaces/phase-00-alignment/02-plans/board-package/01-envoy-concept-one-pager.md` because it is intended for the Foundation board.
**Out of scope:** internal workspace artifacts (`workspaces/phase-00-alignment/01-analysis/*`, `04-validate/*`, `issues/*`) and frozen specs (`specs/*`) — the latter are domain truth, not public marketing copy.

---

## Methodology

Every factual / non-self-evident claim in scope was extracted, classified, and cross-referenced. A claim is in scope if it is:

1. A statement of fact about the world (license, namespace availability, technical capability of a third party).
2. A statement of fact about Envoy's own present or future state (specs frozen, primitives shipped, channels live).
3. A comparative statement about another project (CNCF Envoy Proxy, PyTorch, ring, SQLite, NVIDIA CUDA Python, Linux kernel, Mozilla Firefox).
4. A licensing / legal posture statement that downstream copy will inherit.

A claim is OUT of scope if it is:

- Pure design intent ("the user holds responsibility through Grant Moments") — not a factual claim.
- Internal taxonomy / definition ("an envoy carries delegated authority from a sovereign") — a metaphor, not a claim about the world.
- A direct quote from a Foundation rule / spec (e.g. "Apache 2.0 (code), CC BY 4.0 (specs)") whose authority is already established by Foundation governance.

## Classification dictionary

| Verdict                   | Meaning                                                                                                              | Action                                                                          |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **VERIFIED**              | Internal evidence (file in repo, frozen spec, ADR) supports the claim as written.                                    | None.                                                                           |
| **VERIFIED-EXTERNAL**     | Claim is true at time of writing per public sources, but evidence lives outside this repo.                           | Cite source in companion `references.md` (Phase-00 nice-to-have, not blocking). |
| **UNVERIFIED**            | Claim is not directly checkable from inside this repo and depends on counsel / board / external registry / vendor.   | Block public publication of that surface until external party signs off.        |
| **REWORD**                | Claim is partially correct but the framing over-claims, conflates two distinct items, or hedges a load-bearing fact. | Edit listed file:line; preserve the substance, tighten the wording.             |
| **REMOVE-COMPARATIVE**    | Claim is a comparative statement about a third party that violates `CONTRIBUTING.md` § "Voice and Tone".             | Delete or rewrite as neutral interoperability context.                          |
| **AT-PHASE-DATE-RECHECK** | Claim was true on a stated date in the past and may have decayed; re-verify before public release.                   | Re-run the registry / namespace / availability check immediately before launch. |
